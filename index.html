Hello, World!

☆I grew up in San Mateo, a city I know for its ramen and boba, roughly in between San Francisco and Palo Alto. Though I was surrounded by tech, I was not interested in STEM until attending Girls Teaching Girls to Code right before finishing high school. For the eight years leading up to that, I planned on being a middle school math teacher (I wanted to make math fun and easy for those who, like myself, had struggled with it), whereas my twin brother was the aspiring engineer of the family! I'll never forget how he would receive legos and baseball books, which I enjoyed myself.

☆I'm a Computer Science major, English minor at Bryn Mawr College, where I also play tennis and TA for Intro to CS.

☆I was a developer evangelist intern at PubNub in SF this summer, where I built, wrote, and did cool stuff. To see code I've written, checkout my GitHub, or at the link below.

☆ I'm excited to be joining Twilio this coming summer as an intern on their evangelism team in NYC, working on mobile apps that use their SDKs. If you have any recommendations for places to go and things to see there, let me know! ☆I love musicals, Disney, Harry Potter, Star Wars, running, biking, and tennis (table and regular.)

☆I recently completed a developer-evangelist-in-training program, Clarifai Champions.

☆I help organize events like Spectra, the Bay Area's largest women's hackathon and a series of meetups, and also She++ Code Day, in January, for over 70 students in the Bay Area. I'm currently planning the next one, and am looking for keynote speakers, panelists, and workshop leaders! Use one of my social media links if you're interested :)

Selected Projects, Hacks

*More on GitHub

  • Word-replacing Google Chrome extension(s)

    Can replace some form of "Dodgers" with "the worst team in baseball."

  • Realtime web data visualization of Warriors tweets

    Realtime piechart displaying tweets about the Warriors with PubNub's Twitter Stream and Project EON. Also compares number of tweets about them versus the number of tweets about their opponent.

  • Realtime iOS Heart Rate monitor with Raspberry Pi

    Detect heart rate on Apple Watch and display a realtime bar chart on the iPhone app. Raspberry Pi flashes LEDs based on heart rate data.

  • Realtime Pokemon Go Android map

    Used external API found on Hacker News (no longer maintained) to show realtime map of Pokemon around your current location in Android webview. Used Ngrok.

  • Android Muni map

    Used Uber API, external Muni API, and PubNub's Project EON to display Muni buses around your location and the ability to call an Uber from within the Android app.
