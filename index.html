Lizzie Siegle

"Grass doesn't grow beneath my feet"

I’m a software developer based in SF, currently serving developers on Twilio's Developer Evangelism team. I also help organize Spectra, a hackathon for womxn and non-binary people, and curate tech events, jobs, blog posts, and GitHub repos for my monthly newsletter.

In 2017, I was a Developer Evangelist Intern at Twilio and in 2016 at PubNub. In the spring of 2016, I was an iOS developer intern at Vea Fitness in Philadelphia.

I'm a proud 2018 graduate of Bryn Mawr College in computer science. While there, I played tennis and table tennis, and worked four jobs (in the cafeteria, library, as a Computer Science Teaching Assistant for CMSC110 and CMSC206, and was a CodeHS tutor). For my senior project I developed a series of Django apps and a corresponding iOS app to compare methods of teaching emotions to people on the Autism spectrum. I didn't sleep much.

While in college, I also organized SHE++ Code Day as a SHE++ Ambassador, learned Android with 15 other under-represented minority college sophomores at Twitter Early Bird Camp (building Unagi), learned Ruby at Square Code Camp with 11 other college womxn (building BizzBuzz), and competed in too many hackathons to count (won Best Use of Viacom Data-Point API at Y-Hack 2017).

Selected press includes JS Jabber, Chapter One, Women of Silicon Valley, Technical.ly Philly, Seventeen, Bryn Mawr Athletics, CodeHS Tutors, Reigning It. You can schedule a videochat with me or also email.
